'''Radioactive Man''' is the name of two fictional characters appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]: '''Chen Lu''' and '''Igor Stancheck'''.

==Publication history==
The Chen Lu version of Radioactive Man first appears in ''[[Journey into Mystery]]'' #93 (June 1963), and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Jack Kirby]].

The Igor Stancheck version of Radioactive Man first appears in ''[[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]]'' Vol. 4 #3 (June 2005) and was created by [[Reginald Hudlin]] and [[John Romita Jr.]]

==Fictional character biography==

===Chen Lu===
Chen Lu is a [[nuclear physics|nuclear]] [[physicist]] and [[Communist]] agent in the [[China|People's Republic of China]]. He first appears in ''[[Journey into Mystery]]'' #93 (June 1963). Ordered to find a way to defeat [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] - who thwarts the Red Army's invasion of [[India]] - the character exposes himself to small doses of [[radiation]] until he is able to endure a massive barrage, becoming a living "Radioactive Man". He plans to eventually take over the world.

Traveling to [[New York City]], Chen battles Thor who is first defeated when Chen hypnotizes Thor into throwing the hammer away. But Donald Blake is able to recover the hammer from a river, and defeats the villain by creating a [[vortex]], sending Chen back to China, where he apparently explodes.<ref>''Journey into Mystery'' #93 (June 1963).</ref>

Radioactive Man becomes a recurring foe of the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], being coerced by [[Baron Zemo]] to join the original [[Masters of Evil]] in a bid to destroy the Avengers. He is the first to confront the Avengers, and sticks [[Captain America]] and Giant-Man to the pavement with an adhesive, which Thor dodges. He is defeated by Giant-Man and [[Iron Man]] when they imprison him in [[lead]] coils. He is then deported back to China.<ref>''Avengers'' #6 (July 1964)</ref> The character reappears with the new Masters of Evil, organized by the robot [[Ultron]]. The villains, however, are betrayed by the [[Black Knight (Dane Whitman)|Black Knight]].<ref>''Avengers'' #54 - 55 (July - Aug. 1968)</ref> Radioactive Man and his teammates appear once again during a [[Halloween]] [[parade]] and are defeated by a coalition of superheroines called the [[Lady Liberators]].<ref>''Avengers'' #83 (Dec. 1970)</ref>

Chen escapes and travels to [[Vietnam]], where he joins [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] villains [[Titanium Man]] and [[Crimson Dynamo]] as part of a team called the [[Titanic Three]]. Acting as crime fighters in Vietnam, the trio is duped by a [[confidence trick|con man]] and petty [[thief]] called the Slasher and end up battling the Avengers until the deception is uncovered.<ref>''Avengers'' #130 (Dec. 1974)</ref> After an encounter with Iron Man,<ref>''Iron Man'' #73 - 74 (Mar & May 1975)</ref> the characters go their separate ways. After a humiliating defeat by time-traveling villain [[Kang the Conqueror]] in ''Giant-Size Avengers'',<ref>''Giant-Size Avengers'' #4 (June 1975)</ref> Chen joins another incarnation of the Masters of Evil - led by [[Egghead (Marvel Comics)|Egghead]] - but is defeated by [[Hank Pym|Ant-Man]] and [[deported]] back to China.<ref>''Avengers'' #228 - 230 (Feb. - Apr. 1983)</ref>

Radioactive Man continued to feature in ''Iron Man'', and is employed by Iron Man's arch-enemy [[Mandarin (comics)|Mandarin]].<ref>''Iron Man'' #179 - 181 (Feb. - Apr. 1984)</ref> Chen is employed in his civilian capacity as a physicist by [[Stark Industries|Stane International]] (taken over by [[Iron Monger|Obadiah Stane]]).{{issue|date=June 2012}} Radioactive Man appears in the graphic novel ''Deathtrap: The Vault'', among other supervillains attempting to escape from the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]]. Chen, however, aids the heroes in preventing a nuclear reactor [[nuclear meltdown|meltdown]].<ref>''Deathtrap: The Vault'' (1991)</ref> Radioactive Man also features in an issue of ''Sensational She-Hulk''<ref>''Sensational She-Hulk'' #24 (Feb. 1991)</ref> and once again in ''Avengers''.<ref>''Avengers'' #400 (July 1996)</ref>

After appearing in the second volume of ''Avengers'' that takes place in an [[Parallel universe (fiction)|alternate universe]]<ref>''Avengers'' vol. 2, #8 - 9 (June - July 1997)</ref> and briefly in ''Thunderbolts'',<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #9 (Dec. 1997)</ref> the one-shot ''Heroes Reborn: Masters of Evil'',<ref>''Heroes Reborn: Masters of Evil'' #1 (Feb. 1999)</ref> the third volume of the ''Avengers'',<ref>''Avengers'' vol. 3, #0 (July 1999)</ref> and alternate universe title ''[[Exiles (Marvel Comics)|Exiles]]'',<ref>''Exiles'' #42 (May 2004)</ref> Radioactive Man features in the limited series ''Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes''.<ref>''Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes'' #3 (Feb. 2005)</ref>

Chen attempts to reform and in ''[[Thunderbolts (comics)#The New Thunderbolts|New Thunderbolts]]'' joins the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]], a team composed almost entirely of reformed supervillains.<ref>''New Thunderbolts'' vol. 2, #1 (Jan. 2005)</ref> During the [[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]] storyline, the character aids heroes [[Mister Fantastic]] and [[Hank Pym|Yellowjacket]] in building a holding [[prison]] for dissenting superpowered beings, and also battles the anti-registration heroes.<ref>''Civil War'' #1 - 7 (July 2006 - Jan. 2007)</ref> A major development occurs in the title ''Thunderbolts'' when the group encounters the [[Elders of the Universe]] member the [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]]; the Radioactive Man absorbs an excessive amount of radiation and is forced to wear a radiation suit at all times.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' vol. 2, #106 (Nov. 2006)</ref> The radiation eventually subsides, but industrialist [[Norman Osborn]] convinces the character to retain the suit to distract the American public from his clearly Asian features and quell any lingering fears about his radioactivity.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' vol. 2, #112 (May 2007)</ref> The Radioactive Man is featured in the one shot ''Thunderbolts: International Incident'',<ref>''Thunderbolts: International Incident'' (Apr. 2008)</ref> the limited series ''Avengers/Invaders'',<ref>''Avengers/Invaders'' #1 (July 2008)</ref> one shot ''King-Size Hulk'',<ref>''King-Size Hulk'' #1 (July 2008)</ref> and ''Thunderbolts: Reason In Madness''<ref>''Thunderbolts: Reason In Madness'' #1 (July 2008)</ref> before appearing briefly in the ''[[Secret Invasion]]'' storyline.<ref>''Secret Invasion'' #1 - 8 (Apr. - Dec. 2008)</ref>

As part of the Thunderbolts, Radioactive Man fought [[Spider-Man]] and [[Eddie Brock|Anti-Venom]] during the ''[[New Ways to Die]]'' storyline; during the fight, Radioactive Man had a portion of his radiation powers drained by Anti-Venom.<ref>''Amazing Spider-Man'': "New Ways To Die" #568 - 569 (Oct. 2008); #570 - 572 (Nov. 2008); #573 (Dec. 2008)</ref> He continued with the team however, going after the super-hero [[Moon Knight]].<ref>''Moon Knight'' vol. 4, #24 - 25 (Jan. - Feb. 2009)</ref> Radioactive Man was one of Songbird's strongest allies within the team, often supporting her when she fought against Osborn.

Ultimately, following the events of the ''[[Secret Invasion]]'', Norman Osborn and Moonstone began the process of neutralizing the members of the Thunderbolts who would oppose Osborn in his plan to exploit the invasion for his own personal agenda, in order to gain more power. Osborn arranged for Chen to be deported back to China, leading to a bittersweet departure involving himself and Songbird.<ref>''Thunderbolts'' #126 (Jan. 2009)</ref>

The Radioactive Man reappears in ''[[The Mighty Avengers]]'' as part of [[People's Defense Force]], China's [[indigenous peoples|indigenous]] group of [[metahuman#Marvel Comics|metahumans]]. The Radioactive Man demonstrates that at close proximity he can safely absorb the radiation of Inhuman Xerogen crystals and presumably safely absorb [[Terrigen Mist]]. However, the People's Defense Force is largely defeated by an invading entity known as ''[[The Unspoken]]'', a [[deposition (politics)|deposed]] former king of the [[Inhumans]]. The Unspoken is defeated in China by the Mighty Avengers.<ref>''The Mighty Avengers'' #27 - 28 (Sep. - Oct. 2009)</ref>

During the ''[[Heroic Age (comics)|Heroic Age]]'' storyline, a young man named Warhead appeared as a member of the [[Bastards of Evil]], claiming to be the son of Radioactive Man.<ref>''Age of Heroes'' #2 (Oct. - 2010)</ref> He has been shown to have similar powers, but killed himself in a mass explosion that killed hundreds of people.<ref>''Young Allies'' vol. 2 #1</ref>

Radioactive Man later released a Chinese dragon made of magic in the Federal Bank Reserve. While [[Falcon (comics)|Captain America]], Iron Man, the [[Jane Foster (comics)|female Thor]], and Vision fought the Chinese dragon, [[Spider-Man (Miles Morales)|Spider-Man]], [[Ms. Marvel (Kamala Khan)|Ms. Marvel]], and [[Nova (Sam Alexander)|Nova]] encounter Radioactive Man. The senior Avengers managed to destroy the dragon, but upon reuniting with their wards, they learn they let Radioactive Man escape. The young heroes explain they were forced to leave Radioactive Man alone as there was a man that needed to be rescued. The senior Avengers are proud of what the young heroes did as heroes are meant to protect people first and foremost and welcomes them to the team.<ref>''Free Comic Book Day 2015 Avengers''</ref>

===Igor Stancheck===
Featured in the fourth volume of ''[[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]]'', Igor Stancheck is a [[Russians|Russian]] mutant and is one of several [[mercenaries]] (including [[Rhino (comics)|Rhino]], [[Batroc the Leaper|Batroc]], and the Vatican [[Black Knight (comics)|Black Knight]]), orchestrated by [[Klaw (Marvel Comics)|Klaw]] into invading the fictional [[Africa]]n kingdom of [[Wakanda]]. The group makes an unsuccessful attempt to destroy the huge mound of the metal [[vibranium]] located at the heart of [[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]]'s kingdom.<ref>''Black Panther'' vol. 4 #5</ref> He was killed in battle by [[Shuri (comics)|Shuri]].<ref>''Black Panther'' vol. 4 #6</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Chen Lu was a normal human until exposed to excessive amounts of radiation, which changed his skin coloring to an [[iridescent]] [[green]], and allows him to manipulate radiation across the microwave spectrum. Abilities include radiation emission as heat, "hard" radiation (inflicting opponents with nausea, dizziness, and [[radiation poisoning]]), and [[hypnotic]] light. He can also absorb nuclear radiation without harm and convert it to energy for personal use such as strength enhancement or to [[cauterize]] wounds, create [[force shield|force fields]], and emit electromagnetic radiation to deactivate machinery. He has shown the ability to leave behind "radiation pills", i.e. infusing a single individual with enough radiation to give him severe [[radiation poisoning]] and let the contamination spread in a large population. While it was briefly implied that his radioactive makeup along with some further enhancements of his powers had left him radioactive and unable to have contact with unshielded people, he was [[retcon]]ned to have always been in full control of his abilities. Even so, during his tenure in the Dark Avengers, Norman Osborn had his original costume replaced with a customized radiation suit, hiding his oriental features and quelling every lingering fear about his radioactive makeup. Chen Lu holds a Ph.D. in nuclear physics.

Igor Stancheck has the same powers.

===Television===
* The Chen Lu version of Radioactive Man appears in the Captain America segment of ''[[The Marvel Super Heroes]]'' voiced by [[Gillie Fenwick]].{{citation needed|date=April 2016}} He appears as a member of Baron Heinrich Zemo's Masters of Evil.
* The Igor Stancheck version of Radioactive Man appears in the ''[[Black Panther (TV series)|Black Panther]]'' animated series, voiced by [[Rick D. Wasserman]].{{citation needed|date=April 2016}} He is sent to Wakanda by [[Klaw (Marvel Comics)|Klaw]] and is killed in battle by Princess Shuri.
* The Chen Lu version of Radioactive Man has non-voiced appearances in ''[[The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes]]''.{{citation needed|date=April 2016}} In the episode "Hulk Vs. The World", he makes a cameo as a prisoner of the [[Cube (comics)|Cube]]. He reappears in the episode "The Casket of Ancient Winters", where he fights [[Iron Man]], [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]], and [[Black Panther (comics)|Black Panther]] at [[Stark Industries]] for the Arc Reactor's energy. Radioactive Man is defeated by the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] when Thor blows up the building's water lines, washing out the villain and the reactor. In the episode "Assault on 42", he (along with other villains) volunteers to fight the Annihilation Wave, strategically allowing [[Absorbing Man]] to absorb his radiation, thus they were able to wipe out a massive swarm of the Annihilation Wave. He is apparently killed when the Annihilation Wave crushes a walkway, dropping him into the Negative Zone.
* The Igor Stancheck version of Radioactive Man appears in ''[[Avengers Assemble (TV series)|Avengers Assemble]]'',<ref>{{cite episode|title=Secret Avengers|series=Avengers Assemble|network=Disney XD|season=2|number=17|airdate=May 10, 2015}}</ref> voiced by [[Roger Craig Smith]]. This version is shown to be associated with the [[Winter Guard]] and sports Chen Lu's costume and design. At some point, he was apprehended by [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] and placed in a radiation-proof capsule in order to be used as a power source. In the episode "Secret Avengers", [[Crimson Dynamo]] had obtained the key to the capsule so that Winter Guard could free him. When the destabilized Russian facility threatened a nearby village and the [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]]-affiliated Avengers worked with the Winter Guard to save the village, Crimson Dynamo and [[Falcon (comics)|Falcon]] freed Radioactive Man who then dissolves the facility.
