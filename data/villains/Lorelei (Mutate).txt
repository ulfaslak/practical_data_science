'''Lorelei''' ('''Lani Ubana<ref>''X-Men: Messiah Complex - Mutant Files''</ref>''') is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character's first appearance was in ''X-Men #63''.

==Publication history==
Lorelei first appeared in ''[[X-Men]]'' vol. 1 #63 (December 1969), and was created by [[Roy Thomas]] and [[Neal Adams]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Avengers'' vol. 1 #105 (November 1972), ''Defenders'' vol. 1 #15-16 (September–October 1974), ''Uncanny X-Men'' #104 (April 1977), ''Champions'' #17 (January 1978), ''Super-Villain Team-Up'' #14 (October 1977), ''Marvel Fanfare'' vol. 1 #1-4 (March–September 1982), ''Captain America'' #415-417 (May–July 1993), ''X-Men: The Hidden Years'' #4 (March 2000), ''Cable & Deadpool'' #49 (March 2008), and ''Marvel Comics Presents'' #6-7 (April–May 2008).

Lorelei appeared as part of the "Savage Land Mutates" entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #11.

==Fictional character biography==
Lorelei is a [[Savage Land]] native until [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]] turns her into a mutated siren, whose voice can put men into a [[hypnosis|hypnotic trance]], and she is placed in his [[Savage Land Mutates]] group. However, the machine that Magneto had used to empower his Savage Land creations is eventually destroyed. As a result, Lorelei lost her siren powers.<ref>''X-Men'' #63</ref>

Some time later, she was seen with her powers somehow restored.<ref>''[[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]]'' #15–16</ref> She made a further appearance with the [[Brotherhood of Mutants|Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]].<ref>''[[Champions (1975 team)|Champions]]'' #17</ref>

Years later, Lorelei was seen allied with the High Technician in the Savage Land.  She battled [[Captain America]] and attempted to hypnotize the [[Falcon (comics)|Falcon]], but was quickly defeated by [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]].<ref>''Captain America'' #415 (May 1993)</ref>

Much later, she manipulated [[Ka-Zar (comics)|Ka-Zar]] to battle [[Deadpool]]. This was part of a mission to expand the dominion of the Savage Land. She uses many of the Land's inhabitants. Deadpool shoots her prehistoric ride and leaves her falling towards a group of dinosaurs. She survives this incident.<ref>''Cable & Deadpool'' #49 (March 2008)</ref>

Lorelei helps chase out invading mercenaries.<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #6-7 (April–May 2008)</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Lorelei can hypnotically paralyze men by singing, or manipulate them and place them under her control.

===Ultimate Marvel===
Lorelei has appeared in the [[Ultimate Marvel|Ultimate universe]] as a member of the [[Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]].  She hypnotized [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] into attacking [[Valkyrie (Marvel Comics)|Valkyrie]].<ref>''Ultimates 3'' #2</ref>

She later passes herself off as the mother of the delusional terrorist [[Multiple Man]]; both are working for Magneto. The two are killed by [[Ultimate Wolverine|Wolverine]] in the [[Savage Land]] during [[Ultimatum (Ultimate Marvel)|Ultimatum]].<ref>''Ultimate X-Men'' #100</ref>
